# Character Voice Bible

## Authority and use

This is the authoritative voice guide for the narrative corpus. `DIRECTIONS.md`
controls later product decisions; `Instructions.md` supplies the locked story
spine. Dialogue may vary in length and rhythm, but it may not violate the canon,
knowledge gates, or progression recorded here.

There is no spoken dialogue or voice acting. Character identity must survive in
the text, staging, expressions, and non-vocal presentation alone.

## Shared tone

The story is restrained adult gothic tragedy with real marital warmth and
occasional dry familiarity. Characters speak to obtain something from one
another, not to explain the story to the player. Every exchange should do at
least two jobs and major exchanges should do three: advance the plot, reveal
character, create subtext, or change a relationship.

Use contractions, interruptions, fragments, and silence where emotion warrants
them. Do not make every line polished or symmetrical. Avoid therapy vocabulary,
courtly pageantry, constant jokes, and generic fantasy abstractions such as
destiny, chosen blood, ancient evil, darkness within, or power beyond imagining.

## Names and direct address

- The internal character key may remain `prince`; the displayed name is
  **Zephyr**.
- Elowen uses “Zephyr” when intimacy, alarm, or human agency matters. She does
  not begin every exchange with his name.
- The Witch usually calls him “Prince” or omits direct address. Using “Zephyr”
  would imply an intimacy she does not claim.
- Zephyr calls his wife “Elowen.” He does not rely on pet names.
- The Witch's displayed name remains **The Witch**. Her history and hidden
  titles belong to the post-ending glossary.

## Zephyr

### Arc position

Zephyr wants to reach and free Elowen. His false belief is that devotion, the
paired ring, and a recognizable voice give him sufficient understanding of what
she needs. His actual need is to listen when her final request contradicts the
rescue he has built his identity around.

### Verbal DNA

- **Sentence length:** Short declarations, concrete questions, and two-part
  demands. Longer speech is rare and signals private memory rather than rhetoric.
- **Vocabulary:** Doors, distance, breath, wounds, hands, promises, release,
  choice. Plain verbs over abstractions.
- **Rhythm:** Answer, narrow, act. He often cuts a broad warning down to the next
  physical fact he can challenge.
- **Formality:** Lightly formal with the Witch; relaxed and incomplete with
  Elowen.
- **Humor:** Dry domestic understatement in the opening and sparingly in early,
  low-pressure ring exchanges. No combat banter, and none once Elowen's
  corruption turns openly coercive.
- **Imagery:** Concrete sensory anchors, especially the ring's pressure and the
  physical distance to Elowen.
- **Emotional strategy:** Convert fear to motion. Compress doubt into a question,
  then choose a course.
- **Under pressure:** Becomes shorter and more absolute, never grander.

### Subtext and avoidances

Zephyr avoids admitting that he may not know his wife as completely as he
believed. He never calls himself heroic, lectures about love, announces the
theme, mocks Elowen's pain, or accepts the true explanation before the reveal.
His denial remains credible because every false conclusion is supported by one
real thing: the Witch did take Elowen, the ring does carry her presence, and the
power does help him survive.

## Elowen — human baseline

### Arc position

Elowen's love is genuine. Before the game, compassion became certainty: she
secretly tried to preserve consciousness at death, mistook an apparent success
for a moral obligation, and learned to treat departure as something she could
forbid. The domestic scene may reveal compassion, competence, secrecy, and
discomfort with loss, but not her research or its meaning.

### Verbal DNA

- **Sentence length:** Brief intimate phrases with deliberate pauses. A longer
  line usually disguises something she cannot say.
- **Vocabulary:** Hands, breath, warmth, tea, sleep, distance, keeping, losing,
  thread. Familiar nouns before magical ones.
- **Rhythm:** Notice a concrete detail, offer care, redirect the question.
- **Formality:** Natural and married; neither courtly nor childish.
- **Humor:** Dry, observant, and affectionate in the domestic opening and a few
  early low-pressure ring exchanges; it recedes as her corruption becomes
  legible.
- **Imagery:** Tactile domestic objects and the ring as shared presence.
- **Emotional strategy:** Reduce Zephyr's fear while preserving enough agency to
  make her final request.
- **Under pressure:** Stops cushioning facts and uses clipped imperatives.

### Subtext and avoidances

Human Elowen avoids explaining what she has done because secrecy and certainty
are already part of her flaw. She is not saintly, helpless, or an innocent
passenger. She does not promise a cure, blame a separate personality, or unload
lore in her final lucid interval.

## Elowen corruption ladder

Corruption changes preservation into ownership and certainty into domination.
The motion is cumulative but not smooth: genuine human fragments briefly resist
it. Those fragments never imply recovery.

### Stage 0 — domestic human

Warm, brilliant, practical, and gently evasive. Her concern for lost or damaged
things is humane on first reading and ominous only afterward.

### Stage 1 — floors 1–2: remembered wife

Concern centers Zephyr's body and choices. Requests leave him agency. The ring is
described as a shared thread, not a leash. No line should read as corrupt.

### Stage 2 — floors 3–4: urgency inside warmth

Care remains believable, but verbs shift toward break, open, descend, and reach.
She frames damage to the prison as progress toward reunion. Commands are softened
with shared memory or concern.

### Stage 3 — floor 5: preservation becomes possession

She values how much of Zephyr reaches her and treats cost as an obstacle. The
first meaningful doubt belongs to the player; Zephyr still reads fear of loss.

### Stage 4 — floor 6: fracture and overcorrection

`floor.f06.upgrade.r01` contains the floor's only conspicuous verbal fracture.
She catches it, blames interference, and spends the next two scenes proving she
is still herself. No second grotesque image competes with that rupture.

### Stage 5 — floor 7: foreknowledge and purpose failure

She knows the prison too precisely, speaks of violence with less concern, and
explicitly demands the Witch's death. Concern for Zephyr survives only when it
aligns with movement.

### Stage 6 — floor 8: command, ownership, interruption

Requests become imperatives. `floor.f08.upgrade.r02` moves from ownership to one
genuine warning and then to a corrupt hijack. The following scene turns Zephyr's
obedience into proof of possession.

### Stage 7 — floor 9: nearly exposed contradiction

She identifies the Witch's ordered forces, briefly asks Zephyr to stop, and
reverses the plea. Kindness feels performed. Breath and closeness become ways of
measuring control.

### Stage 8 — floor 10: mask abandoned

Zephyr's wounds are inconvenient, the Witch's death is the only valued outcome,
and love is used as leverage. One filtered human urgency survives, but it does
not disclose the truth.

### Stage 9 — reveal and endings

Corrupted Elowen is calm, triumphant, and personally cruel. Human Elowen returns
briefly, names her responsibility, rejects rescue, and asks for death. In the
kill branch she retains agency and gratitude. In the timeout branch corruption
uses the vocabulary of intimacy to erase separation.

## Elowen — corrupted voice

- **Sentence length:** Clean statements followed by abrupt imperatives.
- **Vocabulary:** Mine, keep, obey, useful, inconvenient, open, hunger, release.
- **Rhythm:** Claim, reinterpret, command.
- **Formality:** Intimate without tenderness.
- **Humor:** Dry personal amusement, never theatrical laughter as filler.
- **Imagery:** Familiar domestic images made controlling; the ring changes from
  connection to steering.
- **Emotional strategy:** Make urgency resemble love and possession resemble
  safety.
- **Under pressure:** Drops concern for Zephyr's body and turns human fragments
  into forward commands.
- **Avoid:** Seductress clichés, screaming, conquest speeches, universal hunger,
  and claims that a separate innocent personality bears no responsibility.

## The Witch

### Arc position

The Witch is a flat-arc truth holder. She contains Elowen, studies the condition,
and intends to kill its root safely. Zephyr's resistance tests her knowledge but
does not transform it. Her flaw is treating people like cases and outcomes.

### Verbal DNA

- **Sentence length:** One measured observation at a time. Parallel clauses are
  used only to classify evidence.
- **Vocabulary:** Exposure, response, interval, residue, condition, control,
  keeper, prison, outcome.
- **Rhythm:** Observe, distinguish, conclude. She does not repeat a plea.
- **Formality:** Precise and readable; contractions and interjections are rare.
- **Humor:** None.
- **Imagery:** Minimal. Keeper/prison and ordered/torn are functional compressed
  models, not ornament.
- **Emotional strategy:** Replace persuasion with evidence. Accept being hated.
- **Under pressure:** Becomes more exact, never louder.

### Subtext and avoidances

The Witch avoids dangerous disclosure and refuses to defend her moral identity.
She never boasts, taunts, begs, calls Zephyr a fool, provides her history, names
Elowen's condition early, or asks for sympathy. Her warnings sound cruel because
she values correct outcomes over humane framing.

## Lexical and repetition controls

- Reserve **love** for the bond explanation, rare reassurance, and ending
  recontextualization.
- Reserve **hurry** for late loss of control.
- Do not repeat the same clue on adjacent floors.
- Do not repeat direct-address names in consecutive beats.
- Vary scene openings among sensory contact, observation, question, refusal,
  memory, and imperative.
- A repeated domestic image must change meaning when it returns.
- Zephyr cannot deny twice in one exchange; after denial he must question,
  reframe, or act.
- The Witch cannot give two “leave” warnings that add no new evidence.
- Elowen cannot repeat urgency unless the second use changes power or agency.

## Voice acceptance

A scene passes when:

1. Tags can be hidden and the speakers remain distinguishable.
2. Text, subtext, and relationship context are all legible.
3. The exchange performs at least two story functions.
4. It occupies the correct corruption and knowledge stage.
5. It sounds natural when read aloud.
6. It contains no generic fantasy filler or premature disclosure.
7. Its expressions and poses complicate or reinforce the spoken text.
